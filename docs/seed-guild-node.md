# Seed Guild Node

## Overview

A **Seed Guild Node** is the basic operating unit of the Seed Infrastructure Protocol.

It represents a place where:

- seeds are stewarded
- knowledge is recorded
- community governance occurs
- stewardship events are documented
- ecological memory is preserved

A guild node may exist within:

- a farm
- a seed library
- a community garden
- a research station
- an indigenous stewardship network
- a regional seed collective

The node is both **physical and digital**.

It connects living seeds, human stewards, and shared infrastructure.

---

## Core Components

Every Seed Guild Node includes five primary components.

### 1. Seed Steward Community

The heart of a guild node is a group of **active stewards**.

Stewards may include:

- farmers
- gardeners
- seed librarians
- plant breeders
- indigenous knowledge keepers
- ecological researchers
- community volunteers

Their responsibilities include:

- growing seed crops
- maintaining varietal integrity
- observing ecological adaptation
- documenting stewardship activity
- sharing seed with other nodes

Human stewardship remains the foundation of the system.

---

### 2. Living Seed Collections

Each guild node maintains **living seed collections**.

These may include:

- locally adapted varieties
- heritage or landrace seeds
- experimental breeding populations
- regionally significant crops
- wild relatives and ecological plants

Seeds are maintained through:

- seasonal cultivation
- regeneration cycles
- careful storage
- exchange with other guild nodes

Seeds themselves act as **biological memory**, carrying adaptation across generations.

---

### 3. Stewardship Records

Guild nodes maintain structured records of stewardship activity.

These records may include:

- seed regeneration events
- planting and harvest notes
- adaptation observations
- exchange events
- stewardship contributions
- environmental observations
