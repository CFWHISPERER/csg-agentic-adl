# Proof of Stewardship (PoS)

Seed Infrastructure Protocol  
Version: 0.1-draft  
Status: Experimental

---

## Overview

Proof of Stewardship (PoS) is a mechanism for recognizing and recording
verifiable contributions to the Seed Commons.

Rather than measuring financial profit or ownership, Proof of Stewardship
records actions that preserve, renew, and expand the living capacity of
seed systems and ecological knowledge networks.

PoS enables distributed networks of stewards to coordinate their work,
recognize contributions, and maintain transparent records of ecological
care across bioregions.

---

## Core Principle

Proof of Stewardship is the verifiable record of actions that preserve,
renew, document, or distribute the living capacity of the seed commons.

---

## Stewardship Events

Proof of Stewardship records are derived from stewardship events.
These events represent real-world actions taken by seed stewards,
guild members, and ecological participants.

Examples include:

### Seed Stewardship

- seed_saved
- seed_shared
- seed_received
- seed_sown
- germination_observed
- variety_preserved

### Ecological Stewardship

- pollinator_habitat_planted
- soil_improvement_recorded
- biodiversity_patch_restored
- climate_observation_recorded

### Knowledge Contributions

- accession_documented
- adaptation_note_added
- lineage_recorded
- training_provided

### Commons Participation

- seed_exchange_facilitated
- council_service_completed
- protocol_contribution_made
- stewardship_event_verified

These event types may expand as the protocol evolves.

---

## Validation Levels

Stewardship events may be validated at different confidence levels.

Validation ensures that the Proof of Stewardship system remains credible
while remaining accessible to decentralized participants.

Possible validation levels include:

- self_reported
- peer_confirmed
- guild_confirmed
- sensor_supported
- multi_source_verified

Validation level influences the confidence and weighting of
stewardship records.

---

## Stewardship Dimensions

Stewardship contributions are evaluated across several dimensions.

These dimensions help recognize the diversity of contributions
within ecological systems.

Example dimensions:

### Continuity
Maintaining a seed lineage or ecological process over time.

### Diversity
Preserving genetic diversity and rare or regionally adapted varieties.

### Adaptation
Supporting locally adapted plants and ecological resilience.

### Sharing
Distributing seeds, knowledge, or resources to others.

### Documentation
Recording knowledge, lineage, and ecological observations.

### Ecological Support
Actions that improve habitat, soil health, or pollinator networks.

These dimensions allow the system to recognize meaningful
contributions beyond simple quantity.

---

## Stewardship Ledger

Proof of Stewardship records may be aggregated into a
bioregional commons ledger.

The ledger records:

- stewardship events
- validation status
- stewardship dimensions
- participating stewards
- bioregional context

The ledger does not represent ownership or financial balances.
Instead, it reflects ongoing contributions to the commons.

---

## Role of Agents

Software agents may assist with:

- validating event structure
- linking events to seed accessions
- identifying duplicate or inconsistent records
- summarizing stewardship contributions
- detecting ecological patterns

Agents assist but do not replace human stewardship governance.

Final interpretation and dispute resolution remain with
guilds and councils.

---

## Relationship to Governance

Proof of Stewardship records may inform governance processes.

Possible uses include:

- identifying active stewards
- recognizing long-term contributors
- informing council participation
- prioritizing ecological support
- detecting neglected varieties or regions

PoS records are advisory signals rather than direct sources
of governance authority.

---

## Relationship to Value Exchange

Proof of Stewardship is not a currency.

However, stewardship records may support voluntary exchange
systems such as:

- seed access reciprocity
- training opportunities
- tool sharing
- collaborative breeding projects
- stipends or grants
- optional tokenized systems

These mechanisms may operate independently from the PoS ledger.
