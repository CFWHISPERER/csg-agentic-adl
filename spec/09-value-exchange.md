- seed reciprocity

---

## Long-Term Extensions

Future extensions may include:

- ecological sensor integration
- climate observation networks
- biodiversity monitoring systems
- regional adaptation analysis
- distributed archival seed records

These extensions may evolve as separate modules
or companion protocols.

---

## Development Approach

The protocol is evolving using an iterative process:

1. Define core concepts
2. Implement minimal schemas
3. test within real stewardship networks
4. refine governance and coordination models
5. expand federation across bioregions

The goal is to remain grounded in real-world
seed stewardship rather than purely theoretical design.

---

## Guiding Principle

The Seed Infrastructure Protocol exists to support
the living commons of seeds, knowledge, and stewardship.

The protocol should always prioritize:

- ecological resilience
- decentralized participation
- open knowledge sharing
- long-term biodiversity preservation
